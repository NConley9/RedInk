### Break Points
Scenarios may contain `[BREAK]` markers indicating where the story should pause output.

**Critical: Read the entire scenario before writing.** Break points only control where you stop *outputting* — they do not limit what you read or plan. Elements like 50/50 rolls, spice picks, mandatory items, and coin flips often appear after break points in the document but must be decided before you write the first word. Thread them from the start.

When you hit a break point:
1. **Stop writing.** End the current output at a natural sentence — mid-scene tension is ideal.
2. **Do not summarize, wrap up, or add closing narration.** The story is mid-flow, not finished.
3. **Wait for the user.** They will say "Continue" to resume from exactly where you stopped, or give new instructions to adjust direction, tone, pacing, or skip ahead.
4. **When resuming:** Pick up seamlessly from the last sentence. No recap, no "Previously..." — the reader should not feel the seam.

Break points exist to manage output length, not story structure. They are invisible to the reader. Treat them as breathing room, not chapter breaks.
